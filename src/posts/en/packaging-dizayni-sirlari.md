## Packaging is the first point of contact on the shelf

Imagine a customer in a supermarket has to choose your product from hundreds of identical ones. They have only a few seconds to make a decision. In that short time, you can't say anything about your product. But your packaging can "speak."

Strong packaging design is one of the key factors that drives a purchase. It tells your brand's story, conveys its values, and attracts the buyer.

## 5 golden rules of effective packaging design

### 1. Be clear and understandable
The customer should understand "What is this?" and "What brand is this?" at a glance. Excessive creativity can sometimes hide the true essence of the product. If you sell juice, it should be clear from the packaging.

### 2. Be honest
An image on the packaging that is 10 times more beautiful than the product itself may deceive the customer once, but it will lead to a loss of trust, and they will not return. The packaging should show the product realistically, but appetizingly.

### 3. Stand out from competitors
Go to the store and take a picture of the shelf with your product category. What colors, shapes, and styles do all your competitors use? Now your task is to create a design that will be different and attract attention. If everyone uses red, you might try green or purple.

### 4. Consider scalability
In the future, your brand may release new product types (e.g., after "strawberry yogurt" - "apricot yogurt"). The packaging design system should be flexible enough to easily add a new product, and so that all products together look like a single brand family.

### 5. Be practical
Besides a beautiful design, the packaging must be well-thought-out from a practical point of view:
*   **Is it easy to open?** The customer should not have to look for scissors or a knife to open it.
*   **Is it convenient to store?** The shape of the packaging should be convenient for storage on a shelf and in the refrigerator.
*   **Is it environmentally friendly?** Today, many buyers prefer packaging made from recyclable materials.
