Once you launch a successful business, it's only a matter of time before "fake" products using your name appear on the market. This situation is extremely common in Uzbekistan. In this article, we explore how to protect your brand **like a shield**.

## What Happens If Competitors Steal Your Name?

If your brand is not officially registered, you may face the following problems:
1. **Loss of customers:** Buyers purchase a low-quality product with a similar name, thinking it's yours.
2. **Reputation damage:** If the counterfeit's quality is poor, the blame falls on your brand.
3. **Financial loss:** Your advertisement campaigns costing millions of UZS are effectively working for a competitor.

## How to Protect Yourself?

### 1. Register a Trademark
This is the primary and only legal path. Only the certificate from the Ministry of Justice confirms you as the true owner of the name.

### 2. Control Your Domain and Social Media
Immediately reserve the `.uz` domain and Instagram/Telegram accounts under your brand name. If you have a patent, reclaiming your name on social media through a complaint ("Report") or court is much easier.

### 3. Ongoing Monitoring
Continuously watch for new brands appearing on the market that look suspiciously similar to your logo or name. Early action means cheaper resolution.

## What If Theft Already Occurred?

If your brand is patented and someone uses it illegally:
- An official warning letter is sent through our agency.
- A complaint is filed with the Intellectual Property Department of the Ministry of Justice.
- The process of holding the offender administratively or criminally liable begins.
