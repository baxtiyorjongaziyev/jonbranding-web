Many entrepreneurs spend thousands of dollars creating a logo. But they don't think about legally protecting it. The result? A competitor can use that logo or create something similar — and you can't do anything about it.

## What Does It Mean to Patent a Logo?

In Uzbekistan, the primary way to protect your logo is to register it as a **Trademark**. This gives you:

- ✅ Exclusive rights to your logo
- ✅ Competitors cannot use it
- ✅ 10 years of protection (renewable)
- ✅ Increased market value of your brand

## "But the Copyright for the Logo is Mine!"

Many people think this way: "I paid the designer, so the logo is mine." That's true — but only from a copyright perspective. **Trademark rights** are a completely different matter.

Copyright:
- Arises automatically
- Protects only the specific image
- Difficult to prove in court

Trademark:
- Only arises upon registration
- Protects even from similar logos
- Guaranteed by the state

## When Should You Register?

**As early as possible.** Uzbekistan operates on a "first to file" principle. This means whoever files first gets the rights to the logo — even if you created it years ago.

Real case: A restaurant operated under its logo for 3 years. Then a competitor registered a similar mark. The result — the restaurant was forced to completely redesign its logo.
