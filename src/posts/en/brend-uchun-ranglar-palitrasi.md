## Color Psychology: What does each color mean?
Each color evokes specific meanings and emotions. It is important to consider this when choosing colors for your brand.

*   **Red:** Passion, energy, urgency. Suitable for brands related to food, sports, and discounts. (Coca-Cola, Netflix)
*   **Blue:** Trust, stability, professionalism. Widely used in technology, finance, and healthcare. (Facebook, Samsung, Intel)
*   **Green:** Nature, health, growth. Suitable for eco-products, healthy lifestyles, and finance. (Starbucks, Android)
*   **Yellow:** Optimism, youth, attention-grabbing. Usually used to express happiness and positive emotions. (McDonald's, IKEA)
*   **Orange:** Friendliness, confidence, call to action. Suitable for creative and energetic brands. (Amazon, Fanta)
*   **Purple:** Luxury, creativity, wisdom. Ideal for premium products and creative services. (Cadbury, Milka)
*   **Black:** Power, elegance, luxury. Often used by premium and fashion brands. (Nike, Chanel)

## How to choose colors for your brand?

1.  **Define your brand's essence:** What emotions should your brand evoke? Is it serious and professional or young and cheerful?
2.  **Study your target audience:** What colors do your customers pay attention to? Their age, gender, and culture affect color perception.
3.  **Analyze your competitors:** Study what colors your competitors use. You need to stand out, but also avoid colors that are alien to your category.
4.  **Use the 60-30-10 rule:** A successful color palette usually consists of three colors:
    *   **60% Primary color:** Expresses the main emotion of your brand.
    *   **30% Secondary color:** Complements the primary color and creates contrast.
    *   **10% Accent color:** Used to grab attention, for call-to-action (CTA) buttons.

Remember, colors are one of the most powerful and memorable elements of your brand. Choosing them strategically can significantly strengthen your brand.
